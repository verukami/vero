Enterprise Healthcare Platform

Simplifying complex workforce and compensation workflows in healthcare systems

Product Design Enterprise UX Accessibility Complex Systems

Large healthcare organizations rely on accurate workforce data to ensure proper compensation, compliance, and operational efficiency.

This project focused on improving a critical internal platform used by healthcare professionals to report working hours, which directly impacts payroll calculations, hospital budgeting, and workforce planning.

Due to confidentiality constraints, this case study focuses on process, decisions, and outcomes rather than final UI visuals.

Context

The platform supports healthcare systems by centralizing workforce operations, including provider management, compliance, and time tracking.

One of its core workflows allows doctors to log worked hours across highly variable schedules, often involving different roles, locations, and approval states.

The Challenge

Time tracking in healthcare is uniquely complex. Physicians do not follow fixed schedules, and errors in reporting can directly affect compensation and operational decisions.

  • Highly complex, data-dense tables with multiple states and time categories
  • High cognitive load for users operating under time pressure
  • Risk of errors impacting payroll accuracy and hospital budgets
  • Strict business and data constraints typical of enterprise healthcare systems
How might we reduce cognitive overload and error risk in a critical, high-stakes time reporting workflow?

My Role

I worked as a Product / UX Designer, focusing on improving usability, clarity, and accessibility within an existing enterprise system.

My scope centered on analyzing and redesigning a complex table-based workflow used by healthcare professionals to log, review, and validate worked hours.

UX Approach

Given the constraints of an enterprise environment, the approach prioritized incremental improvements with high impact.

  • Audited existing workflows to identify friction and error-prone interactions
  • Analyzed information hierarchy within dense tables
  • Reviewed accessibility gaps against WCAG standards
  • Worked closely with product and engineering to validate feasibility

Key Design Decisions

  • Progressive disclosure: Reduced cognitive load by surfacing summaries first, with detailed data available on demand.
  • Visual grouping: Improved scanability of tables through clearer grouping, spacing, and hierarchy.
  • Error prevention: Clarified editable vs locked states to reduce accidental input errors.
  • Accessibility-first: Ensured contrast, focus states, and keyboard navigation supported diverse user needs.

Outcome & Impact

The redesigned time tracking experience enabled healthcare organizations to accurately register working hours across multiple hospitals while reducing manual errors and operational friction.

The system supported complex calculations related to shifts, variable schedules, and compensation, while remaining compliant with accessibility standards and internal workflows.

By improving clarity and structure within a highly constrained environment, the platform became easier to operate for both medical staff and administrative teams.

Why it was complex

This was not a simple data entry table.

The experience needed to support:

  • Multiple hospitals operating under different rules
  • Integration with check-in and check-out systems
  • Automatic hour calculations and validations
  • Data consistency for billing and payroll processes
  • Accessibility requirements for a wide range of users

Each design decision had downstream impact on finance, operations, and compliance, making precision and clarity critical.

Working within constraints

The product relied on an existing design system and legacy technical constraints.

My role focused on translating operational and business requirements into a usable interface without increasing cognitive load for users who interacted with the system daily.

This required close collaboration with product, engineering, and operational stakeholders to ensure the solution was both usable and technically viable.

Reflection

Designing complex operational tools reinforced the importance of clarity over aesthetics.

In healthcare environments, good UX is not about delight. It is about trust, accuracy, and reducing the risk of human error.

This project strengthened my ability to design for systems where usability directly impacts financial and operational outcomes.
