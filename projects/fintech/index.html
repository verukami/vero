Enterprise Digital Banking Platform

Adapting and optimizing banking flows on a modular enterprise platform

Product Design Enterprise UX Design System Banking

This project was developed within a large-scale digital banking platform used by multiple financial institutions across different markets.

The core product, design system, and base application flows already existed for both desktop and mobile experiences.

The challenge was not to redesign from scratch, but to adapt complex financial flows to specific business, regulatory, and user needs without compromising scalability or consistency.

The challenge

While the platform provided robust banking functionality, real-world implementations required adjustments to align with each institution’s operational model.

Many existing flows needed refinement to support local requirements while remaining aligned with the core product architecture.

How can enterprise banking flows be customized to local needs without fragmenting the product or increasing long-term complexity?

Users and context

The end users varied depending on the market and type of banking product, but shared a common expectation of clarity, reliability, and efficiency.

  • End users performing frequent financial operations
  • Internal banking teams managing and configuring products
  • Stakeholders operating under strict regulatory constraints
In enterprise banking, predictability matters more than novelty.

UX approach

The UX approach focused on identifying friction within existing flows and proposing improvements that balanced usability, technical feasibility, and business rules.

  • Reviewing existing desktop and mobile flows
  • Collaborating with banking stakeholders to understand local needs
  • Designing solutions within the constraints of the existing design system

Every proposal required alignment across design, product, engineering, and business.

The platform supported both B2C and B2B banking flows, requiring different levels of complexity, permissions, and operational logic.

Working within a design system

The platform relied on an established design system shared across multiple markets.

My role involved determining when existing components could be reused and when extensions were necessary to support new requirements.

All adaptations were documented to ensure future scalability and consistency.

Cross-functional collaboration

The team included a product manager, business analyst, engineering lead, designer, and additional technical stakeholders.

I worked closely with both internal teams and banking representatives to validate assumptions, align on constraints, and ensure design decisions were feasible and clearly communicated.

Outcome

The resulting solutions enabled banks to adapt critical flows to their needs without deviating from the core platform.

This approach reduced implementation friction while maintaining a consistent user experience across markets.

Reflection

Designing for enterprise banking reinforced the importance of working within constraints rather than around them.

The value was not in visual novelty, but in making complex systems clearer, more adaptable, and easier to operate.
