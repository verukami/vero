Melifloz

Designing a digital space for authentic connections and expressive community experiences

Product Design Community Emotional UX Design System

Purchasing high-value fashion online comes with a specific type of friction: uncertainty.

For customers buying premium Indian garments that are rich in texture, embroidery, and cultural detail. Traditional e-commerce fails to communicate fit, drape, and presence.

Melifloz was created to bridge that gap.

A mobile commerce platform that combines curated, high-end Indian fashion with virtual try-on technology, helping users make confident purchase decisions before checkout.

The Challenge

Traditional fashion e-commerce relies heavily on static imagery and size charts, which are insufficient for garments with complex silhouettes and materials.

  • High return rates due to poor fit expectations
  • Low confidence when purchasing premium-priced items
  • Limited emotional connection to the product
How might we help users visualize how high-value garments will look and feel on their own bodies — before committing to a purchase?

Understanding the Users

Research and behavioral analysis revealed three core user segments:

  • Fashion-conscious buyers looking for premium, culturally rich garments and willing to invest when confident in the fit
  • Occasion-driven shoppers purchasing outfits for weddings, celebrations, or special events
  • Remote customers without access to physical boutiques
Confidence, not discounts, was the main conversion driver.

UX Strategy

The UX strategy focused on reducing purchase hesitation by making the product feel tangible in a digital environment.

  • Clear product storytelling through visuals and details
  • Virtual try-on as a confidence-building tool, not a novelty
  • Minimal steps between exploration and purchase
Information architecture designed to support fast decision-making

Virtual Try-On Experience

The virtual try-on feature allows users to visualize garments directly on their bodies using their device camera.

Rather than aiming for hyper-realism, the experience prioritizes proportion, silhouette, and movement — the key elements users rely on to assess fit.

Reflection

Melifloz reinforced that emerging technologies like AR only create value when they solve a real business problem.

In this case, virtual try-on was not about innovation for its own sake, but about enabling confident, informed purchase decisions in premium fashion.
